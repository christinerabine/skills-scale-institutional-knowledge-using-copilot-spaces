# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze data, document processes, and ensure that projects deliver measurable business value.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements and user stories
- Analyze data to support decision-making and measure outcomes
- Create process maps and workflow diagrams
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure projects align with business objectives
- Provide data-driven insights for prioritization and decision-making
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Improve process efficiency and effectiveness

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user stories
- Data analysis reports and dashboards
- Business case and ROI calculations
- Stakeholder presentations and workshops

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on defining success metrics and validating problem statements with data
- **Project Managers**: Provides requirements clarity and helps define acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Translates business requirements into technical specifications and validates implementations
- **UX/UI Designers**: Works together to ensure designs meet business requirements and user needs

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create intuitive, accessible, and visually cohesive user experiences. They conduct user research, design interfaces, and ensure products are both functional and delightful to use.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Develop and maintain design systems and style guides
- Ensure accessibility compliance (WCAG standards)
- Collaborate with developers on design implementation
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered, accessible designs
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction
- Maintain consistency across product experiences
- Balance business goals with user needs

### Typical Communication
- Design specifications and style guides
- User research findings and personas
- Prototypes and design mockups
- Usability test reports and recommendations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on defining user flows and feature requirements
- **Business Analysts**: Aligns designs with business requirements and user stories
- **Developers**: Works closely on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- **Support/Customer Success**: Gathers feedback on user pain points and usability issues

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and monitoring systems that enable rapid, reliable software delivery. They bridge development and operations through automation and best practices.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code (IaC) and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure security best practices in deployment processes
- Automate repetitive operational tasks
- Respond to production incidents and performance issues
- Maintain disaster recovery and backup procedures

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable deployments
- Maximize system uptime and reliability
- Reduce manual operational overhead through automation
- Ensure scalable and cost-effective infrastructure

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure documentation and runbooks
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Performance and cost optimization recommendations
- Release and deployment status updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides tooling and pipeline support; collaborates on deployment strategies
- **Project Managers**: Reports on deployment timelines and infrastructure dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Integrates testing into CI/CD pipelines and maintains test environments
- **Support/Customer Success**: Provides production logs and system health information during incidents

---

## Support/Customer Success Lead

### Role Summary
Support/Customer Success Leads ensure customers derive maximum value from the product. They manage customer relationships, escalate critical issues, gather product feedback, and drive adoption and retention.

### Responsibilities
- Own customer escalations and ensure timely resolution
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback for product improvements
- Create and maintain customer-facing documentation and knowledge base
- Track customer health metrics and identify at-risk accounts
- Conduct customer onboarding and training sessions
- Advocate for customer needs in product planning discussions

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce time to resolution for customer issues
- Drive product adoption and expansion
- Provide actionable customer insights to product and engineering teams

### Typical Communication
- Customer escalation reports and resolution updates
- Product feedback summaries and feature requests
- Knowledge base articles and help documentation
- Customer health reports and retention metrics

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Provides customer insights and prioritizes feature requests
- **Developers**: Escalates bugs and works on reproducing customer issues
- **DevOps Engineers**: Collaborates during production incidents affecting customers
- **Project Managers**: Communicates customer commitments and timeline expectations
- **UX/UI Designers**: Shares customer usability feedback and pain points

---

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- These roles work collaboratively across the project lifecycle, with clear handoffs and shared accountability for project success.
